Red Fort


About Us:


The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a historic fort in Delhi, India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century. It served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for over 200 years and is now a significant historical monument and tourist attraction. The fort is known for its massive red sandstone walls and its role in Indian history, including its use as a site for Independence Day celebrations

  • It's situated in Old Delhi, adjacent to an older fort called Salimgarh.
  • The Red Fort was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2007, recognizing its cultural and historical importance.
  • The Red Fort is a major tourist attraction and a popular site for cultural events and historical tours.

    • Vision

    The Red Fort is celebrated as the pinnacle of Mughal artistry, a testament to Shah Jahan's vision of exquisite refinement. The brilliance of its design is credited to the esteemed architect Ustad Ahmad Lahori, who also crafted the iconic Taj Mahal.

    Mission

    The Red Fort Complex was constructed to serve as the palace fort of Shahjahanabad, which was the new capital of India during the reign of Shah Jahan, the fifth Mughal Emperor.

    History

    The Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan was responsible for the construction of the Red Fort, which was intended to function as the palace fort for his capital city of Shahjahanabad. When he moved his capital from Agra to Delhi, one of the first things he did was commission the construction of a fort on the banks of the Yamuna River.The original name of the building was Qila-i-Mubarak, which literally translates to “the Blessed Fort.” Over the course of its more than 300-year history, the fort has been home to a wide variety of people, such as Aurangzeb, Jahandar Shah, Muhammad Shah, and Bahadur Shah.
